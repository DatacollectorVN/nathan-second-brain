## Definition
**PRM (Process-supervised Reward Model)** — a [[Reward Model]] that predicts the correctness of *each reasoning step* in a solution. A common solution-level score is the product of per-step correctness probabilities (probability that every step is correct).

## Context
Trained via [[Process Supervision]]. In [[Let's Verify Step by Step]] (2023) the best PRM solved 78.2% of a 500-problem [[MATH]] subset (best-of-1860), beating the [[ORM]] (72.4%) and majority voting (69.6%).

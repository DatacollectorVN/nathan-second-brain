## Definition
**Direct Preference Optimization** — a fine-tuning method that learns from pairs of (preferred, rejected) responses directly, without training a separate reward model like RLHF does.

## Context
Introduced by Rafailov et al. (2023). Now standard in modern alignment and increasingly used for behavioral shaping (e.g., preferring concise outputs over verbose ones).

## Definition
**Active learning** — a data-collection strategy that selects the *most informative* examples to label next, instead of labeling uniformly at random, to get more model improvement per labeling dollar.

## Context
In [[Let's Verify Step by Step]], labelers are shown **"convincing wrong-answer"** solutions (rated highly by the current [[PRM]] yet reaching a wrong final answer), since the PRM is provably mistaken about at least one step. This selection is estimated to be **~2.6× more data-efficient** than uniform labeling.

## Definition
> A property of a search algorithm: it is **complete** if it is guaranteed to find a solution whenever one exists (and to report failure when none does).

## Context
One of the four performance criteria used across the lecture (with [[Optimality]], time, and space). [[Breadth-First Search]] is complete when [[Branching Factor]] b is finite; [[Uniform-Cost Search]], [[Iterative Deepening Search]] and (with a consistent heuristic) [[A* Search]] are complete. [[Depth-First Search]], [[Depth-Limited Search]] and [[Greedy Best-First Search]] are not complete in general.

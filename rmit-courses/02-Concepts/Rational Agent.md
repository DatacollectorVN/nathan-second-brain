## Definition
> An agent that does "the right thing" — where the right thing is that which is expected to maximise goal achievement, given the available information.

## Intuition
The "acting rationally" approach to AI: instead of asking whether a system thinks or behaves like a human, ask whether it acts to best achieve its goals given what it knows. This is the framing modern AI generally adopts.

## How It Works
Grounded in [[Rationality]]: the agent selects actions expected to maximise goal achievement given available information. Connects to economics (utility, decision theory) among AI's foundational disciplines.

## Variants & Depth
> One of the four approaches to AI (acting rationally). Expect this to be developed further with formal agent/environment models and utility/decision theory later in the course.

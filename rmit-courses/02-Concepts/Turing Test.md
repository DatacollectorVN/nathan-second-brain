## Definition
> A test, introduced by Alan Turing in his 1950 paper *Computing Machinery and Intelligence*, of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behaviour equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.

## Intuition
If a human interrogator cannot reliably tell whether they are conversing with a machine or another human, the machine is said to have passed. It operationalises "intelligence" as convincing human-like behaviour.

## How It Works
The machine must *behave* like a human well enough to fool an interrogator. Critiques raised in the course: behaving like a human is not the same as *thinking* like a human, and the test gives little guidance on *how to build* an intelligent system — motivating [[Rationality]] as an alternative target.

## Variants & Depth
> Corresponds to the "acting humanly" approach among the four approaches to AI. Revisit alongside cognitive modelling ("thinking humanly") and the [[Rational Agent]] framing.

## Definition
> The sister method of [[Monte Carlo Simulation]] used for simulating workflows, event networks, and other processes that MCS's formulation-based approach is not adaptable to.

## Intuition
> MCS excels at pushing random inputs through a formula; DES instead models entities flowing through a process over time (queues, servers, events) — think of the airline-catering trolley pipeline rather than a spreadsheet formula.
